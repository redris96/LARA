<Content>Charming courtyard hotel Tucked away in a traditional dusty Beijing hutong, LuSongYuan has all the character and colour that's conspicuously lacking in the blander modern hotels. For starters, it's in an old Chinese mansion constructed around a lovely open central courtyard, rather than a high-rise. It's tastefully furnished throughout with wooden furniture and colourful decorations. In a superior room you get a Chinese-style four-poster bed with gauze curtains. Nb it's not by any means a luxury hotel so obviously can't compete with the swanky Beijing internationals in terms of facilities, vast breakfast buffets, ultra-modern bathrooms etc. But for the money it's great value (there's apparently a dorm for budget travellers though we didn't see this), and very charming and atmospheric. The young staff on reception were pleasant and helpful and many of them spoke good English (none of which was necessarily the norm). There are a couple of computers for internet access (for a fee). The hotel restaurant does decent food, helpfully displayed in a photo album menu. It's in a handy location for the scenic Back Lakes area of town and traditional Hutong neighbourhoods. And even though Beijing can be a bewildering city to navigate, especially on foot, we managed to walk into the centre of town to the main, southern Forbidden City gate in under 25 minutes.
