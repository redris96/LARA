<Content>A very odd place The trouble with themes is just that you can't help it being subjective. Say you were an opera buff and one of your clients mistakenly assumed you liked hard rock concerts and took you to one. You would necessarily put a brave face to what it might turn out to be the most excruciating couple of hours of your life, you may even - if you are so brave - pretend to know something about the band that was playing, but in the end you would certainly be glad to get back to your pad and the first thing you'd do (once you regained your hearing) would be to reach for your collection of opera CDs, playing your favourite piece to restore your inner balance. This is exactly the trouble with the Hollywood Media Hotel. If you are a movies buff you are in your element. If, like me, you just watch movies but couldn't give a hoot about actors, actresses and the fickle world of celluloid, then you would be somewhat less appreciative of this hotel's main features. But let's face it, themed hotels belong to theme parks and although some of our most touristy cities in the world may very well be on the verge of turning into branches of the Disney corporation, I have fortunately failed to notice the presence of hotels pretending to have a locally related theme in all of them, let alone the theme of a place thousand of miles from where you are staying. So, the Hollywood Media Hotel can only get away with its theme in a quirky city like Berlin, and in the middle of the Ku'Damm and right next door to a fast food joint, just to add to the authenticity of all that is gimmicky and tacky. By now, you are either completely turned off and are seeking another hotel, or you are desperate to know more about this place. Let's say that its main feature is the Hollywood posters, plastered on every inch of available space around the public areas. Large chandeliers and various other props add to the phoney effect and greet you on arrival (or was it the staff?). If you had a long flight you may be rudely awaken by all these of images screaming for attention, which is exactly what cinema posters are supposed to do. The short trip to your room through the labyrinthine corridors garishly furnished with more posters, and a carpet reminding you of the hotel name around every corner, takes to what is a total contrast to the rest of this establishment, since the rooms are surprisingly and disappointingly bland. Just when you had resigned (or fired) yourself to the idea that for one night or two you were going to move into a Hollywood set, you are instead rudely projected back to the reality of basic 4 star hotels and are therefore returned to galaxy blandness. (There must be a college somewhere for interior designers specialising in this kind of decor - if anyone knows I would be grateful for the information!) With the only exception of a picture of the thespian to whom the room is dedicated to (did I forget to mention that?), there is absolutely nothing else in your room to even hint that you are staying in a themed hotel. In fact, there is little else to remind you that you are staying in a 4 star hotel, since the hotel management has seen it fit to diligently remove every single item of complimentary toiletries from the bathroom, but saying thatm should you feel the urge to get a toothpaste, they would be delighted to offer it to you if you visited reception. How many people may actually bother to ask for their basket of goodies is unknown to me. If you are like me and you'd rather brush your teeth with the soap out of the dispenser, before having to dress up again to get a few floors down to reception to ask for some toothpaste, I suspect the hotel manages to save quite a lot of money with this stunt. Did I omit to say there is of course a bed, a table, a TV and the rest of the stuff you'd normally associate with a hotel room? I am sorry. Of course you do get all that and you don't even have to go down to reception to ask for it. Was the room clean? Certainly. Was the bathroom OK? Well, yes and no, the shower in my room was powerful enough, but the water took ages to drain, which is a sure symptom of either dodgy plumbing or of a blockage with unspeakable remains of previous guests. I promise you, however, that I failed to ascertain which of these options it was, deciding to leave this pleasure to the next guest. As I hinted at the beginning you either love this place or hate it. There is nothing wrong with its basic ingredients, and I have to give it to the staff that they are very obliging and welcoming, but it's just like a movie set, scrape under the surface and you are not left with very much at all. If you seek calm and sophistication look elsewhere.
