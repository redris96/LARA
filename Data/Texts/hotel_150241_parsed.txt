By Tuesday, there were no rental cars in San Juan. I finally got one, but ended up paying a premium for the car. If you need a car, reserve in advance.My room was on the main floor with a view of the pool through a window. The room was very clean, the air conditioning worked well, and the furnishings were good. Found the room to be a bit musty. Safety deposit box in the closet (with key) and card swipe to get into the rooms. The pool and hot tub are in a courtyard - small but seemed nice. Maid service was every day and the bed sheets were turned down upon my arrival in the evenings. Washroom was clean with plenty of fresh towels.The Hotel was a 2 block walk from the beach with the Intercontinental ($$$) and Hotel El San Juan ($$$) right across the street. The beach there was nice, fairly empty, no waves, the water was warm with a bit of sea weed. I found numerous sand dollars in the water. There were a number of people kite sailing on the beach as it was quite breezy there.There is a shortage of parking in general in Isla Verde beach area. You have to pay to park at restaurants, etc. Parking at the hotel costs $5/day (you take a ticket when you enter, get the front desk to validate your ticket when leaving and feed your ticket into the machine on the way out).Front desk staff at the Hampton Inn were extremely courteous and friendly. One night, I drove into Old San Juan (25 minute drive with some heavy traffic in the Condodo area). The front desk staff recommded a number of nice restaurants and told me where to park + gave me a tourist road map and a little sheet with driving instructions. I ate at the Dragonfly restaurant - small, dark, romantic little restaurant in Old San Juan - had lamb chops and a rice dish that were to die for - expensive, but worth the trip. On my arrival back at the hotel, the front desk staff asked me where I ate and how I liked my meal. They even knew who my server was!The Hampton Inn has a free internet cafe with printers and a wireless WIFI internet system at the hotel. Internet access was free and you could access it from your room, the hotel lobby and even the pool bar! I borrowed a wireless card for my laptop ($100 refundable deposit) from the front desk. This enabled me to keep in touch with home and keep up with my email each evening. Local calls are free, but I was told it would cost about $5/minute to call Canada - expensive!It sounds like the jets from the airport fly right over the pool (very loud) but it didn't really bother me.There are several fast food restaurants (ie. Church's chicken, Ponderosa steak house, etc.) and well as reasonably priced restaurants (Mi Casita?) and expensive restaurants (ie. Ruth Chris steakhouse at the Intercontinental Hotel) within walking distance of the hotel. I walked to the restaurants at night - didn't feel uncomfortable walking at night. It seems that there is a police car patrolling the street every 2-3 minutes.The hotel offers a well stocked, free continental breakfast every morning (juice, milk, cereal, baked goods, yogurt, fresh fruit, toast and bagels, eggs and sausage).Expect traffic jams driving in San Juan in the mornings unless you get an early start (around 7:00am).Overall, a reasonable hotel with very nice front desk staff.
